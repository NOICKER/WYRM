# WYRM Beginner Guide

This guide is written for someone who has never played WYRM before and does not already know the game words.

It explains the game in the order a new player experiences it:

1. what the words mean
2. what gets set up
3. what happens on a turn
4. what happens when you move
5. what happens when you capture, promote, use tiles, and win

If the rulebook feels dense, read this first.

## 1. What WYRM Is

WYRM is a strategy board game played on a **12×12 grid**.

Each player controls **3 snake-like pieces**. In the game, those pieces are called **Wyrms**.

You are trying to win in one of two ways:

- **Race win:** get **2 of your Wyrms** into the center of the board at the same time
- **Capture win:** capture **all 3 original Wyrms** from one opponent

The special twist in WYRM is that when a Wyrm moves, it leaves behind **trail markers**. Those trails block movement later. So every move helps you now, but may trap you later.

That is the core idea of the whole game.

## 2. What The Game Words Mean

Before anything else, here are the important words in plain English.

### Wyrm

A **Wyrm** is one of your main playing pieces.

If you ever feel lost reading the rules, mentally replace:

- “Wyrm” with “my snake piece”

Each player starts with **3 regular Wyrms**.

### Elder Wyrm

An **Elder Wyrm** is an upgraded Wyrm.

It is stronger because it:

- can move diagonally
- can choose to move 1, 2, or 3 spaces instead of following the die distance
- leaves shorter-lasting trails

You do not start with an Elder on the board. You earn it later.

### Den

A **Den** is your home corner.

Your 3 starting Wyrms begin in your Den.

Later in the game, if you captured enemy Wyrms, you can place them into your own Den and use them as your own pieces.

### Sacred Grove

The **Sacred Grove** is the **2×2 area in the center of the board**.

It is one of the two ways to win.

If you ever get **2 of your Wyrms in the Sacred Grove at the same time**, you win immediately.

### Trail

A **trail** is a marker placed on a square after a Wyrm leaves that square.

Trails are extremely important because:

- a Wyrm cannot move through a trail
- a Wyrm normally cannot end on a trail
- this includes your own trails

So trails are both:

- a way to control space
- a danger you create for yourself

### Hoard

A **Hoard** is your captured-piece area.

When you capture an enemy Wyrm, it goes into your Hoard.

Later, instead of moving normally, you may place one of those Hoarded Wyrms into your own Den and use it under your control.

### Rune Tile

A **Rune Tile** is a power card.

You draw Rune Tiles during the game and may play one on your turn.

Each type of tile gives a different effect.

### Lair Power

A **Lair Power** is a stronger effect made by discarding **3 matching Rune Tiles**.

So:

- 1 tile = small effect
- 3 matching tiles = big effect

### Power Rune Spot

A **Power Rune Spot** is a special marked square on the board.

If your Wyrm lands there, then on your **next Draw step** you draw **2 Rune Tiles instead of 1**.

That bonus only happens once for that spot. After it is used, that spot is no longer special.

### Wall

A **Wall** is a permanent blocked square created by Earth powers.

Nothing can move through it.

## 3. What Is On The Board

The board has several different kinds of spaces.

### Open terrain

Most of the board is just normal open squares.

### The 4 Dens

Each player has a **2×2 Den** in a corner.

- top-left
- top-right
- bottom-left
- bottom-right

These are where players begin.

### The Sacred Grove

The center 2×2 area is the Sacred Grove.

That is the main race goal.

### Power Rune Spots

There are **8 special Power Rune spaces** around the center.

They give the next-draw bonus described above.

## 4. What Gets Set Up Before The Game Starts

This is the order of setup.

1. Place the board.
2. Place the Sacred Grove marker in the center.
3. Place Power Rune tokens on the 8 Power Rune spots.
4. Shuffle the Rune Tile deck.
5. Each player chooses a color.
6. Each player takes:
   - 3 regular Wyrms
   - 1 Elder token
   - trail markers
   - 1 Hoard tray
7. Each player places all 3 regular Wyrms in their own Den.
8. Each player draws 4 Rune Tiles.
9. Choose the first player.

At this point, the game begins.

## 5. What Happens On Every Turn

Every turn always happens in the same 4-step order.

1. **Draw**
2. **Roll**
3. **Move**
4. **Play a Tile** (optional)

If you remember this structure, you can always orient yourself during play.

## 6. Step 1: Draw

At the start of your turn, you draw Rune Tiles.

### Normally

You draw **1 tile**.

### If you earned a Power Rune bonus

If one of your Wyrms landed on a Power Rune spot earlier, then this Draw step becomes:

- draw **2 tiles instead of 1**

Then that Power Rune bonus is used up.

### Hand limit

You may never keep more than **5 tiles** in hand.

So if drawing would bring you above 5, you must immediately discard down to 5.

You choose which tiles to discard.

### Why this step matters

Draw is how you:

- gain one-time tactical powers
- build toward 3 matching tiles for a Lair Power

## 7. Step 2: Roll

After drawing, you roll the custom Rune Die.

This die tells you how much movement you must use this turn.

### Normal numbered faces

- `1` = move exactly 1 space
- `2` = move exactly 2 spaces
- `3` = move exactly 3 spaces
- `4` = move exactly 4 spaces

### Surge

- `Surge` = move exactly 5 spaces

This is the longest normal move.

### Coil

- `Coil` = choose one of these:
  - move 1 space
  - move 2 spaces
  - move 3 spaces
  - or do not move and place an extra trail on an empty adjacent square

### Important note about Elder Wyrms

You still roll even if you have an Elder Wyrm.

But if you choose to move an Elder, the Elder does **not** have to obey the rolled distance. The Elder may move **1, 2, or 3 spaces freely**.

## 8. Step 3: Move

This is the most important step in the game.

On Step 3, you choose **one** of these:

- move one Wyrm on the board
- or deploy one Wyrm from your Hoard into your Den

You do **not** do both.

### Option A: move a Wyrm

You choose one Wyrm you control on the board and move it.

### Option B: deploy from your Hoard

Instead of moving, you may take one Wyrm from your Hoard and place it onto an empty square in your own Den.

That deployed Wyrm is now under your control.

No trail is placed when you deploy this way.

## 9. How Normal Movement Works

A regular Wyrm follows these rules.

### It moves orthogonally

That means:

- up
- down
- left
- right

Not diagonally.

### It must use the full distance

If you rolled 4, then your Wyrm must move **exactly 4 spaces**.

You cannot stop early unless a special rule says otherwise.

### It cannot reverse into the square it just came from

The game tracks your Wyrm's previous position.

This prevents cheap back-and-forth movement.

### It cannot cross trails

Normally, a Wyrm cannot:

- move through a trail
- end on a trail

That includes:

- your trails
- enemy trails

### It cannot cross walls

Walls are permanent blocked squares.

### It cannot move through your own Wyrms

Friendly pieces block each other.

### It can capture by landing

If the final square of your move contains an enemy Wyrm, that enemy is captured.

You cannot pass through an enemy in the middle of the move. Capture only happens if the enemy is your final square.

## 10. How Elder Movement Works

An Elder Wyrm is different from a regular Wyrm.

### An Elder may:

- move orthogonally or diagonally
- choose to move 1, 2, or 3 spaces freely

### An Elder still may not:

- move through walls
- move through friendly Wyrms
- cross blocked spaces unless a power allows it

### Why Elders are strong

An Elder is better at navigating crowded late-game boards because:

- diagonals open more routes
- shorter free movement helps avoid overcommitting
- Elder trails disappear much sooner

## 11. What Happens After A Wyrm Moves

As soon as a Wyrm finishes moving, several checks happen.

### First: trail placement

Every square the Wyrm left behind becomes trail.

If the Wyrm moved through 4 squares, that means multiple trail markers may appear.

The final square does **not** become trail, because the Wyrm is still standing there.

### Exception: your own Den

Your own trail does not remain in your own Den.

This makes it easier to leave and re-enter your home space.

### Second: capture

If your final square contained an enemy Wyrm, that enemy is captured.

### Third: Power Rune check

If your final square was a Power Rune spot, you earn the extra draw on your next Draw step.

### Fourth: promotion check

If your regular Wyrm ended in an enemy Den, it may become an Elder.

### Fifth: victory check

The game checks right away whether this move won the game.

## 12. How Trails Age And Disappear

Trails do not last forever.

### Normal trail duration

A regular Wyrm's trail lasts **3 full rounds**.

That means it slowly ages and then disappears.

### Elder trail duration

An Elder's trail lasts only **1 round**.

That is one reason Elders are easier to maneuver.

### Serpent trail duration

Some Serpent effects make one Wyrm's trail last **5 rounds**.

### Why trail timing matters

A path that looks closed now may open later.

Strong players constantly ask:

- which trails are about to disappear?
- which routes will open next round?

## 13. What Happens When You Capture

A capture happens when your Wyrm lands on an enemy Wyrm as its final square.

### Exact result of a capture

1. The enemy Wyrm is removed from the board.
2. It goes into your Hoard.
3. Your Wyrm takes that square.
4. The game checks whether this creates a Domination win.

### Important detail

If the captured Wyrm was an Elder, it loses Elder status when captured.

It becomes a regular Wyrm in the Hoard.

## 14. What You Can Do With The Hoard

Captured Wyrms are not just trophies.

On a future Move step, instead of moving normally, you may:

- choose one Wyrm from your Hoard
- place it into an empty square in your Den

That Wyrm is now controlled by you.

This is why the Hoard matters so much.

Capturing does two jobs at once:

- it removes an enemy from the board
- it may later give you an extra piece under your control

## 15. How Promotion Works

Promotion is how a regular Wyrm becomes an Elder.

### Trigger

If your regular Wyrm finishes its move in **any square of an enemy Den**, it promotes.

### Result

The regular Wyrm is replaced by your Elder token.

### Why it matters

Promotion is powerful, but it also costs time because you are taking a detour toward a corner instead of the center.

So promotion is usually best when:

- the board is getting crowded
- a diagonal mover would be very valuable
- or the route into the enemy Den was already efficient

## 16. Step 4: Play A Tile

After moving, you may choose to play a Rune Tile.

This step is optional.

You may either:

- play **1 normal Rune Tile**
- or discard **3 matching Rune Tiles** for a **Lair Power**

You do not do both in the same turn.

## 17. What Each Normal Rune Tile Does

Here is each tile in plain English.

### Flame

Removes **all of your own trails** from the board.

Use this when your own movement is trapping you.

### Flow

Lets one chosen Wyrm pass through **one trail square** this turn.

Normally it still cannot end on that trail square.

### Stone

Places **one permanent wall** on any empty square.

Use this to block routes.

### Gust

Gives one chosen Wyrm **+2 movement** this turn.

Good for sudden long-range pressure.

### Eclipse

Swaps the positions of **two of your Wyrms that are already on the board**.

This does not create trail by itself.

### Radiance

Lets you look at one opponent's hand.

Use this to learn whether they are preparing a big Lair Power.

### Erasure

Removes up to **3 trail markers from one opponent**.

Use this to reopen blocked lanes.

### Coil

Makes one chosen Wyrm's trail last **5 rounds** instead of 3, and refreshes that longer duration on its existing trail.

Use this when you want to lock down space for a long time.

## 18. What Each Lair Power Does

If you hold 3 matching tiles, you can use a stronger effect.

### Fire ×3: Phoenix Molt

Remove **every trail on the board**.

This resets the board's trail pressure immediately.

### Water ×3: Flood Path

For your next **3 turns**, your Wyrms ignore trails.

This is extremely strong in a crowded board.

### Earth ×3: Fortress

Place **3 walls**.

This can change the entire shape of the board.

### Wind ×3: Tempest Rush

All of your Wyrms on the board each get an extra move of up to 3 spaces this turn.

This can create massive surprise pressure.

### Shadow ×3: Void Walk

Teleport one of your controlled Wyrms, even from your Hoard, to any empty square.

This can instantly create:

- a center threat
- a promotion threat
- or a setup for a capture

### Light ×3: Blinding Flash

One opponent skips their next **2 turns**.

That is a huge tempo swing.

### Void ×3: Annihilation

Remove **all trails of one chosen color**.

### Serpent ×3: Ancient Wyrm

Promote one of your Wyrms instantly into an Elder.

## 19. How You Win

There are two win conditions.

### Win condition 1: Sacred Grove Victory

If you ever have **2 of your Wyrms inside the Sacred Grove at the same time**, you win immediately.

This is checked right away after the move that created it.

### Win condition 2: Domination Victory

If your Hoard ever contains **all 3 original Wyrms from one opponent**, you win immediately.

Again, this is checked right away after the capture that created it.

## 20. Why The Game Checks For Victory Immediately

This matters because a winning action should not wait.

If your move creates 2 Wyrms in the Sacred Grove, you win right then.

If your capture gives you all 3 original Wyrms from an opponent, you win right then.

You do not continue playing out the rest of the round.

## 21. What A First Full Game Usually Feels Like

A first game usually unfolds in a recognizable order.

### Opening

Players leave their Dens and create the first trails.

At this stage, the main challenge is learning how not to trap yourself.

### Early midgame

Players begin contesting:

- Power Rune spots
- safe routes toward the center
- easy captures

### Midgame

This is when the board starts feeling tight.

You begin seeing:

- self-blocking
- trail congestion
- the first strong tile plays
- possible promotions

### Late game

This is when somebody clearly leans into one win condition:

- race the center
- or dominate through captures

Elders and Lair Powers become much more important here.

## 22. Best First-Game Advice

If you are brand new, focus on these ideas.

### Do not just ask “Where do I want to end?”

Also ask:

- “What trail am I leaving behind?”
- “Will that trail trap me next turn?”

### Do not burn tiles for no reason

A tile is strongest when it solves a specific problem.

Examples:

- use Flame when your own trail is the problem
- use Erasure when enemy trail is the problem
- use Gust when extra speed changes the board right now

### Do not ignore the Hoard

A captured Wyrm is not only removed from the enemy.

It may later become your piece.

### Watch both win conditions at all times

Even if you think you are racing, the other player might be close to Domination.

Even if you think you are capturing, the other player might be one move from the Sacred Grove.

## 23. The Simplest Way To Think During Play

On every turn, ask these 4 questions in order:

1. What did I draw, and is it useful now or later?
2. Given this roll, which Wyrm can move without trapping me?
3. If I move here, what trail am I creating?
4. Did this move bring me closer to the center or closer to a capture win?

If you keep asking those, you will understand the game much faster.

## 24. One-Sentence Summary Of WYRM

WYRM is a game where you move snake pieces across a board, leave behind blocking trails, use power cards at the right moment, and win either by getting 2 pieces into the center or by capturing all 3 original pieces from one opponent.
