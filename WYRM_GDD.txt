1. Game Concept & Design Philosophy
WYRM is a 2–4 player abstract strategy board game in which players control a team of three serpents (Wyrms) competing to claim the Sacred Grove at the centre of a 12×12 grid. Every Wyrm leaves a coloured trail of territory as it moves — making the board progressively more dangerous with each turn. Trails block all pieces (including friendly ones), so every decision is simultaneously an advance and a future constraint.
The game is intentionally easier to learn than Chess but richer than its individual inspirations. It achieves this through layered mechanics: the trail system introduces spatial pressure; the tile hand introduces resource management; the Hoard introduces reversibility; and the Elder promotion introduces a power arc without requiring complex piece hierarchies.

1.2  Core Design Tensions
Trail as weapon and cage — the further you move, the more you block yourself.
Racing vs capturing — pursuing Grove victory means leaving your Wyrms vulnerable.
Single tile utility vs set patience — burn a tile now or hold for a Lair Power?
Movement speed vs Elder promotion — a detour to an enemy Den gives lasting advantage at a tempo cost.

2.4  Den Corner Cells (Promotion Triggers)
Any of the 4 cells within an opponent's Den acts as a promotion trigger. When an opponent's regular Wyrm lands on any of these 4 cells, it is immediately promoted to Elder Wyrm. The Den is a 2×2 zone, and all 4 cells are equally valid promotion triggers.

4. Complete Game Rules
4.1  Objective
A player wins by achieving exactly one of two victory conditions:
Sacred Grove Victory — Have 2 or more of your Wyrms inside the Sacred Grove (the 4 central cells) simultaneously at any point during your Move step.
Domination Victory — Capture all 3 Wyrms belonging to any one opponent such that all 3 are simultaneously present in your Hoard tray.
⚠  Victory is checked immediately after each capture or move action. A player can win mid-turn. The game ends the instant a victory condition is met.
4.2  Setup Procedure
Place the board in the centre of play area.
Place the Sacred Grove overlay on cells (5,5), (5,6), (6,5), (6,6).
Place one Power Rune token on each of the 8 PRune spots (§2.3).
Shuffle the 40 Rune Tiles. Place the deck face-down beside the board. Clear space for a discard pile adjacent to the deck.
Assign player colours (Purple / Coral / Teal / Amber). Each player takes: 3 Wyrm tokens, 1 Elder token (set aside — not yet in play), 20 trail markers, 1 hoard tray.
Each player places all 3 of their Wyrm tokens in their corner Den.
Each player draws 4 Rune Tiles as their opening hand.
The youngest player takes the first turn. Play passes clockwise.
4.3  Turn Structure
On every turn, a player must execute these 4 steps exactly in order:
Step 1 — DRAW
Take 1 Rune Tile from the top of the deck and add it to your hand.
→ If you are currently standing on a Power Rune spot: draw 2 tiles instead. Remove the PRune token (permanent).
→ Maximum hand size is 5. If drawing would bring you to 6+, you must discard to 5 immediately — your choice.
→ If the deck is empty: shuffle the discard pile to form a new deck before drawing.
Step 2 — ROLL
Roll the Rune Die (custom d6). Note the result.
→ If your active Wyrm is an Elder: you do NOT use the die roll for movement. Skip mentally to Step 3.
→ All other Wyrms: the die result defines exact mandatory movement spaces.
Step 3 — MOVE
Option A (Move a Wyrm): Move one of your Wyrms on the board exactly the rolled number of spaces. Elders move 1–3 freely.
Option B (Deploy from Hoard): Instead of moving, place one Wyrm from your Hoard tray into any empty cell inside your own Den. No trail is placed. Roll is ignored.
→ You cannot do both Option A and Option B in the same turn.
→ After any capture: check Domination Victory immediately.
→ After any movement: check Sacred Grove Victory immediately.
Step 4 — PLAY TILE (Optional)
You may play exactly one Rune Tile from your hand by placing it face-up on the discard pile and resolving its effect.
→ Alternatively, if you hold 3 identical Rune Tiles, you may discard all 3 to trigger a Lair Power instead (§7).
→ This step is entirely optional. You may pass without playing anything.
→ You may NOT play more than one single tile per turn (only one tile OR one Lair Power, not both).

5.2  Regular Wyrm Movement Rules
All of the following rules apply on every regular Wyrm move:
Orthogonal only — a regular Wyrm may move up, down, left, or right. Never diagonally.
Exact distance — the Wyrm must travel exactly the number of spaces shown on the die (except Coil face). The player may choose any valid path to reach a cell exactly that many steps away.
No reversal — the Wyrm cannot move into the cell it occupied at the start of that move (the cell it just vacated as the first step of movement). It may visit previously occupied cells from earlier turns if those trail markers have since expired.
No trail crossing — the Wyrm's path may not pass through OR end on any trail marker cell, regardless of colour. Trails from all players block all movement.
No friendly stacking — the Wyrm's path may not pass through a cell occupied by another of your own Wyrms. You cannot end movement on a friendly Wyrm.
No wall crossing — the Wyrm cannot enter a Wall tile cell at any point during movement.
Capture on landing — if the Wyrm's final cell is occupied by an enemy Wyrm, that Wyrm is immediately captured (§8).
Path flexibility — the player may choose any valid path of the exact rolled length that respects all constraints. Paths may double back through open cells (not just direct routes), as long as the no-reversal and no-trail rules are observed at every step.
5.3  Elder Wyrm Movement Rules
An Elder Wyrm replaces its regular Wyrm token and operates under distinct rules:
8-directional movement — Elders move orthogonally OR diagonally (all 8 compass directions).
Free distance choice — Elders move 1, 2, or 3 spaces per turn. The die roll is completely ignored for Elder movement. The player freely selects distance (1, 2, or 3) each turn.
Trail duration — trails left by an Elder last only 1 round (not 3), making the Elder far more manoeuvrable.
All other movement rules still apply — no trail crossing, no friendly stacking, no wall crossing, capture on landing.
5.4  Coiled State (Blocked Wyrm)
If, at the start of Step 3, a chosen Wyrm has no legal moves available regardless of die roll:
The Wyrm is declared Coiled.
It does not move this turn.
If at least one adjacent cell (orthogonally adjacent) is empty, the player must place 1 of their own trail markers on one such empty adjacent cell (player's choice which one).
If all adjacent cells are also blocked, no trail marker is placed. The turn ends without movement.
📌  A player may not deliberately choose to Coil a Wyrm that has valid moves. They must select a different Wyrm that can legally move if any exists. Coil only applies when the chosen Wyrm is genuinely trapped.
📌  Using the Coil die face (⊕) to place an extra trail marker is NOT the same as the Coiled state above — ⊕ is a voluntary choice; the Coiled state is a forced non-move due to being boxed in.
5.5  Trail Placement
After a Wyrm completes its movement (but before checking victory conditions):
Place a fresh trail marker of your colour on every cell the Wyrm vacated during this move.
Exception: Do not place trail markers on cells inside your own Den. Den cells never hold trail markers for their owning player.
Trail markers from all other players CAN exist inside your Den — only your own markers are excluded.
Fresh markers are at full opacity. Track their age by noting the game round they were placed.
Trail markers are removed at the end of the third full round after they were placed.
⚠  Trail removal must be tracked per-marker, not per-Wyrm. If a Wyrm moved 4 spaces, those 4 trail markers were all placed in the same round and expire together. But if the same Wyrm moved again the next turn, that turn's markers expire one round later.
6. Rune Tile Specification
6.1  Deck Composition
The Rune Tile deck contains 40 tiles: 8 element types, 5 copies of each. The deck is shuffled once at the start of the game and reformed from the discard pile when empty. Players draw from the top. There are no draft or secret-selection mechanics — draw order is random.
6.2  Hand Rules
Starting hand: 4 tiles.
Draw 1 tile at the start of each turn (Step 1).
Draw 2 tiles if on a Power Rune spot (one-time bonus, removes the PRune token).
Maximum hand size: 5 tiles at any time.
Play 1 tile per turn maximum (Step 4, optional).
Discard excess tiles immediately after drawing (player's choice which to discard).
Played tiles go to the face-up discard pile.
Discarded-for-size tiles also go to the face-up discard pile.
Players may look at the discard pile at any time. The deck is opaque.

Single-use effect: Your chosen Wyrm's trail duration is extended to 5 rounds (instead of 3). 7. Lair Powers — Tile Sets
A Lair Power is triggered during Step 4 when a player holds exactly 3 tiles of the same type in their hand simultaneously. The player discards all 3 tiles to the discard pile and immediately resolves the Lair Power effect. This counts as the Step 4 action — no additional single tile may be played the same turn.
📌  A Lair Power may be triggered immediately after drawing the third matching tile (i.e., on the same turn you complete the set). You do not need to wait until a future turn.
⚠  A Lair Power is OPTIONAL. A player who holds 3 matching tiles is not forced to trigger the power — they may wait, hold them, or even discard one voluntarily (e.g., when over the 5-tile hand limit). However, only 5 copies of each tile exist, so waiting risks losing access to the third copy.

8. Capture & Hoard System
8.1  Capturing a Wyrm
A capture occurs when a Wyrm lands on a cell occupied by an opponent's Wyrm (regular or Elder) as the final cell of its movement.
Remove the captured Wyrm token from the board.
Place the captured token in the capturing player's Hoard tray.
The capturing Wyrm now occupies that cell. Trail markers are placed on all vacated cells, as normal.
Check for Domination Victory immediately: if the capturing player now holds all 3 Wyrms of any single opponent in their Hoard simultaneously, the game ends and they win.
📌  A Wyrm cannot capture a friendly Wyrm. You cannot land on your own pieces under any circumstances.
📌  A Wyrm cannot pass through an enemy Wyrm — it can only capture by landing on that exact cell. Mid-path collisions are not captures.
8.2  The Hoard Tray
Each player has a personal Hoard tray placed in front of them. It holds any Wyrm tokens captured from opponents. There is no limit to the number of Wyrms in a Hoard.
Important Hoard tracking rule: For the Domination Victory, only the original 3 Wyrms of a single target player matter. So if Player 1 has captured two of Player 2's Wyrms but Player 2 previously had one of their Wyrms redeployed (returned to Player 2 via their own Hoard mechanism), then the redeployed Wyrm must also be recaptured for Domination.
8.3  Redeploying Hoarded Wyrms
On Step 3 of your turn, instead of moving, you may deploy one Wyrm from your Hoard into your own Den:
Choose one Wyrm token from your Hoard tray (any player's).
Place it on any empty cell within your own Den (any of the 4 cells in your corner).
This token is now fully under your control — it is one of your pieces.
No trail markers are placed when a Wyrm is deployed from the Hoard into a Den.
The deployed Wyrm is a regular Wyrm, even if it was an Elder when captured. It starts fresh without Elder status.
📌  There is no maximum on how many Wyrms you can field simultaneously. A player could theoretically control their own 3 Wyrms plus captured pieces — making it theoretically possible to have 6+ pieces on the board at once. This adds pressure toward Domination Victory for a player who has been capturing heavily.
8.4  Den Blocking Edge Case
If all 4 cells of a player's Den are occupied (by their own Wyrms, opponent trail markers, or Wall tiles), they cannot deploy from the Hoard that turn. They must move an existing Wyrm instead. If they also have no Wyrms on the board that can legally move, they skip the Move step entirely (rare).
9. Elder Promotion
9.1  Promotion Trigger
Promotion occurs when a regular Wyrm lands on any of the 4 cells belonging to an opponent's Den at the end of a move. The 4 Den cells of each player are defined in §2.1. Any cell within the 2×2 corner zone of an opponent's Den qualifies.
📌  The player whose Den is invaded is NOT penalised beyond the promotion — their Den remains their safe deploy zone and no pieces are automatically captured by the promotion. Promotion and capture are separate events.
9.2  Promotion Procedure
The move lands on an opponent Den cell.
Remove the regular Wyrm token from the board.
Place the owner's Elder Wyrm token on that same cell.
All trail markers placed during this move remain.
The Wyrm is now an Elder from this point forward for this token's lifespan.
📌  Only one Elder token per player exists. If a player already has their Elder on the board, their second Wyrm reaching a Den corner cannot be promoted. They must wait until the Elder is captured or until the Ancient Wyrm Lair Power is used (which allows promoting a Wyrm that is NOT currently an Elder via the special Serpent ×3 power).

10. Win Conditions & Edge Cases
10.1  Sacred Grove Victory
Achieved when a player has 2 or more of their Wyrms occupying Sacred Grove cells simultaneously. The Sacred Grove is defined as cells (5,5), (5,6), (6,5), (6,6).
Checked immediately after any movement (Step 3).
An Elder Wyrm in the Grove counts equally as a regular Wyrm.
A Hoarded-then-Teleported Wyrm (via Shadow Lair Power Void Walk) arriving in the Grove counts.
Wyrms in the Sacred Grove are NOT immune to capture. An opponent can enter the Grove and capture one, removing the victory condition before the winner is declared.
The game ends IMMEDIATELY upon checking — the player wins even if it is mid-turn.
The Sacred Grove can hold up to 4 Wyrms simultaneously (4 cells) — multiple players' Wyrms can coexist in the Grove at once.
10.2  Domination Victory
Achieved when a player holds all 3 original Wyrms of any single opponent in their Hoard simultaneously.
Checked immediately after a capture (Step 3).
A player can achieve Domination over Player 2 even while Player 3 or Player 4 still has Wyrms on the board.
"All 3 original Wyrms" means the 3 tokens that began the game belonging to that opponent. Redeployed opponent tokens count as that opponent's tokens regardless of current controller.
⚠  If an opponent's Wyrm is currently under a different player's control (via Hoard deploy), it is not in the original player's Hoard — so it cannot count toward their Domination unless recaptured.
10.3  Tiebreaker
If two players achieve a victory condition simultaneously (e.g., via a Lair Power affecting multiple boards states), the player whose action directly caused the condition wins.
If still tied: the player with the larger hand (more tiles) wins.
If still tied: the player with more Wyrms on the board wins.
If still tied: a sudden-death turn is played — first player to capture an opponent Wyrm wins.
10.4  Exhaustive Edge Cases
Scenario
Resolution
Player moves into Sacred Grove with 1 Wyrm; already has 1 there
Check: 2 Wyrms now in Grove → Sacred Grove Victory declared.
Player captures Grove Wyrm reducing opponent below 2 in Grove
Recheck victory: opponent no longer qualifies → game continues.
Elder captured in Sacred Grove
Elder goes to captor's Hoard as regular Wyrm. Grove victory check for opponent may fail.
Player's only moves box into walls and trails
Apply Coiled state (§5.4). Must still check if any other Wyrm can move — cannot voluntarily Coil.
Rune Tile deck runs out mid-draw
Shuffle discard pile immediately, form new deck, complete draw.
Player holds 3 matching tiles but deck is empty on their Draw step
Form new deck from discard before drawing. Then check for Lair Power if desired.
Flood Path (Water Lair) active — can Wyrm END on a trail cell?
Yes during Flood Path only. The Wyrm may end its movement on a trail cell. It does not remove that trail marker.
Wall placed on a Power Rune spot
Allowed. The Power Rune token is removed (or was already removed). The Wall tile now occupies that cell.
Can a Wyrm pass through the Sacred Grove without stopping?
Yes. A Wyrm may pass through Grove cells during movement without triggering victory — only the FINAL position triggers the check.
Can a player have 0 Wyrms on the board?
Yes. If all 3 are captured and in opponents' Hoards. They may only play a Tile in Step 4 and must use Deploy if a Wyrm is returned to them via redeployment.
Void Walk (Shadow Lair) used to place a Wyrm in opponent's Den
Allowed. The Wyrm is placed on that enemy Den cell, triggering Elder promotion immediately.
Two players declare Lair Powers on the same turn
Impossible — only one player acts per turn. Lair Powers are declared in Step 4 of the active player's turn only.
Elder uses Wind tile (+2 spaces)
Elder's normal 1–3 range becomes 1–5. Same directional freedom.
Player has 4 Wyrms on board (from Hoard deploy of captured piece)
Fully legal. All movement, trail, and capture rules apply equally.
Can a trail marker be placed on a Sacred Grove cell?
Yes. Trails can cover Grove cells. A Wyrm with a trail on a Grove cell still cannot enter it (trail blocking applies inside Grove).
11. Game State & Data Structures
This section defines the minimal data model for implementing WYRM programmatically. All coordinates are (row, col) with row 0 at top, col 0 at left.
11.1  Board State
type CellType = "open" | "den_p1" | "den_p2" | "den_p3" | "den_p4" | "grove" | "power_rune"
type PlayerId = 1 | 2 | 3 | 4
interface TrailMarker {
  owner: PlayerId;          // which player placed this trail
  placedRound: number;      // game round number when placed
  expiresAfterRound: number;// placedRound + 3 (or +1 for Elder, +5 for Serpent tile)
}
interface Cell {
  row: number;
  col: number;
  type: CellType;
  trail: TrailMarker | null;  // at most one trail marker per cell at a time
  hasWall: boolean;            // permanent wall tile present
  hasPowerRune: boolean;       // power rune token still present
  occupant: WyrmId | null;     // which Wyrm is here, if any
}
type Board = Cell[][];  // 12×12 grid: board[row][col]
11.2  Wyrm State
type WyrmStatus = "active" | "in_hoard" | "in_den";
interface Wyrm {
  id: WyrmId;              // unique identifier e.g. "p1_w1"
  originalOwner: PlayerId; // the player who started with this Wyrm
  currentOwner: PlayerId;  // who controls it right now (changes on capture+redeploy)
  isElder: boolean;
  position: { row: number; col: number } | null; // null if in hoard
  status: WyrmStatus;
  prevPosition: { row: number; col: number } | null; // for no-reversal rule
}
11.3  Player State
interface PlayerState {
  id: PlayerId;
  color: "purple" | "coral" | "teal" | "amber";
  hand: RuneTileType[];       // max 5 tiles
  hoard: WyrmId[];            // captured Wyrms held by this player
  elderTokenAvailable: boolean; // true if Elder token not yet on board
  floodPathTurnsRemaining: number; // 0 normally; counts down from 3 (Water Lair Power)
  skipTurnsRemaining: number;      // 0 normally; counts from Blinding Flash
}
11.4  Global Game State
interface GameState {
  board: Board;
  players: PlayerState[];          // indices 0–3 map to PlayerId 1–4
  wyrms: Map<WyrmId, Wyrm>;
  currentPlayerIndex: number;      // 0–3
  currentRound: number;            // increments after all players have taken a turn
  phase: TurnPhase;
  dieResult: DieResult | null;     // null before roll in current turn
  deck: RuneTileType[];            // top of array = top of deck
  discardPile: RuneTileType[];
  powerRunesRemaining: Set<string>; // "row,col" keys of remaining PRune tokens

  winType: "grove" | "domination" | null;
}
11.5  Turn Phase Enum
type TurnPhase =
  | "draw"        // Step 1: player draws tile(s)
  | "roll"        // Step 2: player rolls die
  | "move"        // Step 3: player selects Wyrm and moves
  | "play_tile"   // Step 4: player optionally plays a tile
  | "end_turn"    // clean-up: expire trails, advance turn
  | "game_over";
11.6  Rune Tile Type Enum
type RuneTileType = "fire" | "water" | "earth" | "wind" | "shadow" | "light" | "void" | "serpent";
type DieResult = 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | "coil" | "surge";
// Mapped to move distance: 1→1, 2→2, 3→3, 4→4, surge→5, coil→player choice 1/2/3 or extra trail
12. Core Algorithms
12.1  Legal Move Generation
To compute all legal destination cells for a given Wyrm and move distance:
function getLegalMoves(wyrmId, distance, gameState): Cell[] {
  const wyrm = gameState.wyrms.get(wyrmId);
  const isElder = wyrm.isElder;
  const dirs = isElder
    ? [{r:-1,c:0},{r:1,c:0},{r:0,c:-1},{r:0,c:1},
       {r:-1,c:-1},{r:-1,c:1},{r:1,c:-1},{r:1,c:1}]
    : [{r:-1,c:0},{r:1,c:0},{r:0,c:-1},{r:0,c:1}];
  // BFS/DFS: find all cells reachable in exactly `distance` steps
  // Each step: cell must be empty of walls, trails (unless Flood Path active),
  // and friendly Wyrms. Previous cell cannot be re-entered (no-reversal).
  // A cell with an enemy Wyrm is a TERMINAL step (capture) — cannot pass through.
  // Return: all unique terminal cells reachable at exactly `distance` steps.
}
12.2  Trail Expiry Logic
At the end of each turn (end_turn phase), before advancing to the next player, run:
function expireTrails(gameState) {
  for each cell on board:
    if cell.trail && gameState.currentRound > cell.trail.expiresAfterRound:
      cell.trail = null;
}
📌  Trail expiry is checked after every turn, not just at end of round. A trail placed on round 5 with expiresAfterRound=8 will be removed at the end of the turn that begins round 9.
12.3  Victory Check
function checkVictory(gameState): { winner: PlayerId, type } | null {
  // Sacred Grove Victory:
  for each player P:
    const groveCells = [(5,5),(5,6),(6,5),(6,6)]
    const inGrove = P's wyrms with position in groveCells
    if inGrove.length >= 2: return { winner: P, type: "grove" }
  // Domination Victory:
  for each active player P:
    for each opponent Q:
      const qOriginalWyrms = all 3 WyrmIds originally belonging to Q
      if all 3 are in P.hoard: return { winner: P, type: "domination" }
  return null;
}
12.4  Trail Placement After Move
function placeTrails(wyrmId, pathCells, gameState) {
  const wyrm = gameState.wyrms.get(wyrmId);
  const owner = wyrm.currentOwner;
  const dur = wyrm.isElder ? 1 : 3; // serpent tile modifier tracked separately
  for each cell in pathCells except the final destination:
    if cell is NOT inside owner's own Den:
      board[cell.row][cell.col].trail = {
        owner,
        placedRound: gameState.currentRound,
        expiresAfterRound: gameState.currentRound + dur
      };
}

17. 2-Player Variant Rules
The standard rules apply in full for 2 players with the following adjustments:
Players use diagonally opposite corners: Player 1 takes (0,0) Den; Player 2 takes (10,10) Den (lower-right).
The other two corner zones are left uncoloured and are treated as open terrain. A regular Wyrm landing on any cell in an empty corner zone is NOT promoted — promotion only triggers on actively used opponent Den cells.
Sacred Grove victory requires 2 Wyrms in the Grove as normal.
Domination victory requires all 3 of the opponent's Wyrms simultaneously.
Average game time: ~30–45 minutes.
Recommended removal: Remove 10 Rune Tiles from the deck at random before playing to increase scarcity. This prevents hand-building being too reliable.
18. 3-Player Variant Rules
Three players use any three of the four corner Dens (chosen before setup). The unused Den:
Remains coloured on the board but functions as open terrain (no Wyrms start there).
A regular Wyrm landing on a cell within the unused Den is NOT promoted.
All other rules apply in full.
